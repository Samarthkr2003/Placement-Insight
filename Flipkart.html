Software Developer
Pre Round: To get a chance to participate in this coding rounds, we were asked to fill forms . the candidates and were short listed here on the basis of pointer ( 7.5 and above were all selected, for people with pointer 7 to 7.5, there projects and internships were taken into consideration to be allowed to appear in this round)
Round 1: Online Assesement Round- 3 CODING questions in 90 mins on hackerrank.
  • Was a simple question, all you had to do was find a PnC formula and just put the values in it to get the answer.
  • Was based on array . This was a medium level question, 7/11 test case passed using brute force (O(n^2)). An accurate solution of which is actually in O (n), but i modified my code and managed to get all the 11 test cases right using just a slight modification in my brute force approach!
  • A matrix was given, the solution to which was by applying BFS on it but not exactly that. I could not understand the problem enough as I was running out of time ! The name of the Question was “Marathon” and said was a lot similar to some other famour problem named “Rat in a maze”.
  • In this round out of 150 students, 22 were shortlisted for interviews, and 10 were kept on waiting list.

Round 2: Techincal Round 1 - In technical round one, all of us were asked 3 questions. The level of questions was actually easy, and most were selected only if they could answer all of the 3 right !
  • N-1 elements in an array from 1 to n, find the missing one!
  • K distance sort He had clearly stated me that this can be done using normal sorting algo in O(nlogn), but I had to give a more optimized one. I proposed a solution using a stack, which some how worked for the test case that he gave me, but he proved it wrong and gave me a hint to use another data structure, I got the hint and realised it was to be done using min heap. Time complexity of which was O(nlogk) !
  • Circular tour - For this question i gave him a more optimized solution than brute force but with the same complexity, but he did not give me much time for this question as they were running late on schedule.


Round 3: Techincal Interview 2 - 17 out of 22 made it to this round.
In this round the questions asked were tough, but the interviewer was very suggestive and kept dropping hints.
  • Sum of minimum elements of all sub arrays.
  • Cut off trees for golf
  • LRU cache implementation using array, stack/queue, doubly linked list, circular linked list, Hash map.


Round 4: HR Interview 2 - Number of selected Students 12
  • Where do you see yourselves in 5 years?
  • What is your strength and weakness? To which I answered as hard working, being both my weakness and strength and justified it .
  • He asked me about my story if JEE and getting into this college. (11 STUDENTS SELECTED IN THE END)
